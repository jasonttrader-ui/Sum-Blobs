Parent Area

Learning Modes

Addition: Children combine blobs to reach a target number, encouraging counting and understanding that numbers can be built from smaller parts.

Parent Tip: Ask your child to count the blocks in each blob before combining them.

Subtraction: Children discover how many remain when one amount is taken away from another.

Parent Tip: Use phrases like "take away" and "how many are left?"

Multiplication: Children learn that multiplication represents equal groups and repeated addition.

Parent Tip: Ask, "How many groups are there?" and "How many are in each group?"

Division: Children practice sharing numbers into equal groups and recognizing division patterns.

Parent Tip: Ask, "If we share these equally, how many does each group get?"

Parent Tips

Learn together: Ask, “How did you figure that out?”

Mistakes help: Encourage trying again instead of rushing.

Use real life: Count snacks, toys, coins, steps, or objects around the home.

Keep it short: A few focused minutes can be more effective than a long session.

Celebrate effort: Praise thinking, practice, and persistence.

Why It Matters

Children often grow more confident when parents take an active interest in learning. Small questions, encouragement, and practice can make math feel less intimidating and more enjoyable.
